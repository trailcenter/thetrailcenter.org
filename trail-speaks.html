Support and Advocacy Branch

Centering advocacy, information, and lived experience

TRAIL Speaks provides trauma-responsive support, workshops, mentorship, resources, advocacy, and lived-experience-centered programming for victims, survivors, offenders, justice-involved individuals, practitioners, families, and communities affected by trauma.

Support Pathways

Resource navigation, mentorship, workshops, support materials, and trauma-responsive guidance for people affected by trauma.

Advocacy and Voice

Programming that centers lived experience, amplifies voice, and supports people in understanding and naming trauma-related harms.

Community Education

Public education, discussion-based workshops, and resources that help communities understand trauma and respond with dignity.

Important Note

Support, not emergency services

TRAIL Speaks provides support resources, advocacy-oriented programming, workshops, mentorship, and education. It does not provide emergency services, crisis intervention, legal representation, or clinical therapy.

If you are in immediate danger or experiencing an emergency, call 911. If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, contact a local crisis line or emergency service in your area.
