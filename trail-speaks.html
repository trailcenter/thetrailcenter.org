TRAIL Speaks

Support and advocacy for people affected by trauma.

Request a WorkshopGet Involved

TRAIL Speaks provides workshops, mentorship, resources, and support pathways for people affected by trauma, including victims, survivors, offenders, justice-involved individuals, practitioners, families, and communities impacted by the criminal justice system.

TRAIL Speaks is grounded in the belief that trauma must be heard, understood, and responded to with dignity. People affected by trauma often move through systems that misunderstand their experiences, reduce their stories to labels, or overlook the harm they carry.

Purpose

The purpose of TRAIL Speaks is to support people affected by trauma by providing access to information, mentorship, workshops, resources, and trauma-responsive guidance.

Who TRAIL Speaks supports

Victims and Survivors

People affected by domestic violence, sexual violence, family violence, or other forms of harm.

Justice-Involved Individuals

Offenders and justice-involved individuals seeking to understand trauma, coping, accountability, and change.

Practitioners and Communities

Criminal justice practitioners, advocates, behavioral health professionals, families, and communities affected by trauma.

What TRAIL Speaks offers

Workshops

Trauma-focused workshops on coping, relationships, systems involvement, healing, accountability, practitioner trauma, and more.

Mentorship

Guidance and connection related to trauma, justice, education, advocacy, or professional growth.

Resources

Educational guides, reflection tools, support resource lists, community resource guides, and practitioner support materials.

Voice and Storytelling

Survivor voice projects, practitioner reflection projects, community listening sessions, awareness campaigns, and speaker events.

Important Note

TRAIL Speaks provides education, mentorship, workshops, resources, and support pathways. It does not replace emergency services, legal representation, therapy, or crisis intervention.

If you are in immediate danger or experiencing a crisis, call 911 or contact a local crisis service.

TRAIL Center

Trauma Research, Advocacy, Impact, and Learning

The TRAIL Center is an independent trauma-focused center dedicated to advancing trauma-responsive systems through research, support, training, and policy change.
