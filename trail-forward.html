Policy and Systems Change

TRAIL Forward

Translating trauma research, lived experience, and practice knowledge into tools for systems-level change.

Collaborate With TRAIL Forward

TRAIL Forward is the policy and systems-change branch of the TRAIL Center. It translates trauma research, lived experience, and practice knowledge into policy guidance, model protocols, organizational recommendations, and systems-change tools.

TRAIL Forward is grounded in the belief that trauma-responsive change requires more than awareness. Systems must examine how policies, practices, procedures, and institutional cultures shape the experiences of people affected by trauma.

Focus areas

Policy Guidance

Research-informed policy guidance for agencies and organizations seeking to improve trauma responsiveness.

Model Protocols

Consistent and effective response protocols for trauma-affected individuals and communities.

Systems-Change Tools

Organizational self-assessments, practice checklists, policy review guides, and implementation tools.

Program Evaluation

Needs assessments, training evaluations, program evaluations, policy implementation reviews, and outcome summaries.

Research-to-Practice Translation

Policy briefs, practice briefs, white papers, agency reports, and systems-change recommendations.

Organizational Consultation

Support for agencies seeking trauma-responsive policies, practices, training, and culture change.

Who TRAIL Forward Works With

TRAIL Forward is designed for agencies, organizations, and communities seeking trauma-responsive policy and systems change.

  • Criminal justice agencies
  • Victim service organizations
  • Behavioral health organizations
  • Domestic violence and sexual violence agencies
  • Courts and legal service providers
  • Corrections and reentry programs
  • Probation and parole agencies
  • Community-based organizations
  • Interdisciplinary coalitions
