Our Work

The TRAIL Center advances trauma-responsive systems through four connected branches: research, support and advocacy, training and education, and policy and systems change.

Each branch has a distinct focus, but they are designed to work together. Research informs training. Lived experience informs advocacy. Training supports better practice. Policy work translates knowledge into systems-level change.

Research

TRAIL Lab

Trauma-focused research on adaptation, coping, victimization, offending, justice involvement, practitioner experiences, systems response, and life-course outcomes.

Learn more

Support & Advocacy

TRAIL Speaks

Workshops, mentorship, resources, storytelling, support pathways, and community-based programming for people affected by trauma.

Learn more

Training & Education

TRAIL Academy

Trauma-responsive trainings, workshops, educational resources, and professional learning opportunities.

Learn more

Policy & Systems Change

TRAIL Forward

Policy guidance, model protocols, organizational recommendations, evaluation, and research-to-practice tools.

Learn more

How the Branches Work Together

Research helps identify problems, patterns, gaps, and possibilities.

Support and advocacy centers the experiences of people affected by trauma.

Training and education translate knowledge into practice.

Policy and systems change move trauma-responsive ideas into organizations, agencies, and institutions.
