About the TRAIL Center

An independent trauma-focused center dedicated to advancing trauma-responsive systems through research, support, training, and policy change.

What TRAIL Stands For

T — Trauma
R — Research
A — Advocacy
I — Impact
L — Learning

Trauma is the foundation of the Center’s work. Research, advocacy, impact, and learning are the pathways through which the Center advances trauma-responsive change.

Mission

The mission of the TRAIL Center is to advance trauma-responsive systems by translating trauma knowledge into research, support, training, education, and policy change.

Vision

The TRAIL Center envisions communities and systems that recognize trauma, respond with understanding, reduce harm, and create pathways toward healing, accountability, and meaningful change.

What We Believe

The TRAIL Center is grounded in the belief that trauma cannot be understood only as an individual experience. Trauma is also shaped by families, communities, institutions, policies, and systems. It influences how people survive, cope, adapt, respond, and move through the world.

The Center recognizes that trauma affects many groups, including victims and survivors, justice-involved individuals, practitioners, families, and communities. A trauma-responsive approach requires more than awareness. It requires research, education, support, accountability, and systems change.

Core values

What guides the work

Trauma-Responsive Practice

We recognize trauma as a central force shaping behavior, coping, harm, healing, and systems involvement.

Human Dignity

People affected by trauma deserve to be seen, heard, supported, and treated with dignity.

Research-Informed Action

Research guides practice, training, policy, and systems change.

Voice and Lived Experience

We value the knowledge of victims, survivors, practitioners, justice-involved individuals, and communities affected by trauma.

Learning and Growth

Education and training can transform how people, organizations, and systems respond to trauma.

Systems Accountability

We examine how institutions can reproduce harm and work toward more responsive, humane, and effective systems.

Founder and Director

The TRAIL Center was founded by Dr. Lori L. Hall, a criminologist whose work focuses on trauma, crime, victimization, maladaptive coping, justice systems, and trauma-responsive change.

Dr. Hall’s work bridges criminology, trauma research, lived experience, teaching, advocacy, and institutional reform. The Center reflects a commitment to transforming trauma knowledge into action.
