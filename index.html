Trauma as the foundation

Research. Advocacy. Impact. Learning.

Trauma shapes lives, choices, relationships, communities, and systems. It affects victims and survivors, justice-involved individuals, practitioners, families, and the institutions responsible for responding to harm.

The TRAIL Center bridges the gap between trauma knowledge and real-world change by bringing together research, support, professional training, education, and policy work.

Transforming trauma knowledge into action.

The Center is built around four branches that work together to create more humane, responsive, and effective pathways forward.

Who we serve

Built for people, practitioners, organizations, and systems

The TRAIL Center serves people and organizations seeking to understand trauma, reduce harm, strengthen practice, and create meaningful systems change.

  • Victims and survivors
  • Justice-involved individuals
  • Criminal justice practitioners
  • Victim advocates
  • Behavioral health professionals
  • Community organizations
  • Agencies seeking trauma-responsive training or policy support
