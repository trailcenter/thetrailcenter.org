Trauma Research in Adaptation, Innovation, and the Life Course

TRAIL Lab

The research branch of the TRAIL Center.

Join as a Research AssistantPartner With TRAIL Lab

TRAIL Lab conducts trauma-focused research on adaptation, coping, victimization, offending, justice involvement, practitioner experiences, systems response, and life-course outcomes.

The Lab is grounded in the belief that trauma shapes how people survive, adapt, cope, and move through systems. TRAIL Lab examines trauma not only as an individual experience, but also as a social, institutional, and life-course force that influences behavior, harm, healing, and systems involvement.

Research Focus

TRAIL Lab studies the relationship between trauma and human adaptation across the life course. The Lab is especially interested in how trauma influences coping strategies, justice involvement, substance use, victimization, offending, practitioner trauma, and institutional response.

Core research areas

Trauma and Adaptation

How individuals adapt to trauma over time and how survival strategies may become misunderstood, pathologized, criminalized, or punished.

Trauma, Coping, and Maladaptive Behavior

How trauma shapes substance use, avoidance, aggression, self-protection, emotional withdrawal, and other responses to harm.

Trauma and Justice Involvement

Pathways into the criminal justice system, including victimization, offending, survival behavior, system contact, and institutional response.

Victimization and Offending

The overlap between victimization and offending and how trauma complicates traditional categories of victim, offender, survivor, and practitioner.

Practitioner Trauma

Trauma among criminal justice practitioners, victim advocates, behavioral health professionals, and others who work with trauma-affected populations.

Systems Response

How institutions respond to trauma and how systems can either reduce harm or reproduce it.

Research Opportunities

TRAIL Lab offers opportunities for Research Assistants and research staff to develop skills and contribute to meaningful trauma-focused projects.

  • Literature reviews
  • Article summaries
  • Survey and interview development
  • Data collection
  • Qualitative coding
  • Quantitative analysis
  • Program evaluation
  • Conference posters and presentations
  • Manuscript preparation
  • Community-based research projects

Partner With TRAIL Lab

TRAIL Lab welcomes opportunities to collaborate with community organizations, agencies, practitioners, researchers, and partners interested in trauma-focused research.

Potential collaborations may include community-based research, program evaluation, needs assessments, practitioner-focused research, survey or interview studies, research translation, conference or publication development, and trauma-responsive systems evaluation.
